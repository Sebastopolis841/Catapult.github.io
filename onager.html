Main

Onagers were powerful and heavy seige machines used by the Romans to hurl masive rocks and stones at enemy fortifications. They were used to break through the walls of enemy fortresses and castles, as well as to destroy enemy fortifications. The onager was a type of catapult that used twisted sinuw or rope to store energy and launch projectiles. The onager was a powerful and effective weapon that was used in many battles.

Stats

Onager:
-Range: 1400 ft.
-Projectile: Stone ball
-Speed: 59 feet per second
-Power: At least 1000 lb of force

How it's built

An onager consists of a large frame of wood, with a wooden pole holding a container passing through a rope bundle which contains a projectile. To launch it, the wooden pull was pulled down and then suddenly released against the tension of multiple ropes and slings, firing the projectile forwards extremely fast.
